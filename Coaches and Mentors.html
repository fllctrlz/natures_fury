Coaches and Mentors

Eric Borromeo

Eric is currently Vice President at a U.S.-based financial institution where he serves as the risk control officer for its Eastern Canada mid-corporate banking business line. Previously, he served in several roles at both Canadian and France-based banks. In his free time, Eric serves as the parent-coach of Bayview Glen's Ctrl-Z FLL robotics team. Since assuming this role in 2009, he has had the honour of accompanying Ctrl-Z to FIRST World Festivals in both Atlanta and St. Louis. Eric also enjoys cycling, alpine skiing, and recently began playing golf.




Alexia Moliotsias

Ms. Moliotsias is very excited to be acting as one of the coaches for the Ctrl-Z team. Ms. Moliotsias is currently a grade 8 Mathematics teacher at Bayview Glen and is coaching an FLL team for the second time. Ms. Moliotsias has an Electrical Engineering Degree from the University of Toronto and completed her Bachelor of Education at the Ontario Institute for Studies in Education (OISE). In her spare time, she enjoys reading, live theatre, film festivals and of course playing with LEGO.





Dobrin Alexiev

Mr. Alexiev has been designing software at Texas Instruments for the past 15 years. His interest in microprocessors, computer languages and user interface frameworks is the key driver for his ongoing contribution to the development of Texas Instruments' Integrated Development Environment - Code Composer Studio. He has MSc in Computer Science from Sofia University, Bulgaria. Outside of work he loves to share his passion for LEGO, computers, mathematics and physics with family and friends. He enjoys skiing, biking and playing volleyball with his two sons, Chris and Alex, who are also big LEGO and FLL enthusiasts.




Patrick Mark

Dr. Mark is currently an Assistant Professor in the Faculty of Medicine at the University of Toronto and helps teach the doctors of tomorrow. He practises medicine at Toronto East General Hospital where he is a staff anesthesiologist. Prior to medical school, he did his undergraduate training in Electrical Engineering. As a child, his favourite activity was taking apart his toys to see how they worked. He now enjoys reliving his childhood as an FLL mentor.
