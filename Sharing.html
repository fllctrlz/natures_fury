Sharing and Recognition

FIRST LEGO League Quick Setup Video - April 13, 2013

On April 13, 2013, at Bayview Glen School, we helped with the filming of a FIRST LEGO League Quick Setup video for rookie teams. To see this video, click here.


Brickfete - July 13, 2013

On July 13, 2013, we presented the robot game and our robot, our research presentation, and increased awareness about FLL at Brickfete, a LEGO fan festival.




Crescent FLL Kick-off Event - September 21, 2013

On September 21, 2013, we presented our robots from the Food Factor and Senior Solutions seasons, some programming, building techniques and explained the robot game rules for this year's challenge, Nature's Fury, during the FIRST LEGO League Kick-off event at the Crescent School.




FIRST Symposium - October 5, 2013

On October 5, 2013 at St. Mildred's-Lightbourn School in Oakville, Ontario, we taught approximately 20 FLL teams advanced programming such as PID line following, how to build passive attachments, and we demonstrated our robot.




FLL Regional Qualifying Tournament - November 30, 2013

On November 30, 2013, we volunteered as referees and scorekeepers at Bayview Glen School for the FLL regional tournament.

Sharing our Solution with Anastasios Venetikidis of the University of Toronto - December 3, 2013

On December 3, 2013, we shared our solution, the Upright Light, with one of our experts, Anastasios Venetikidis.




Presenting at Our School Assembly - December 5, 2013

On December 5, 2013, we shared our research project and innovative solution with our school at its weekly assembly. Approximately 250 students and teachers were in attendance.




Sharing with Electronically Overdressed Survivors ("EOS"), an Alaskan FLL Team - January 11, 2014

On January 11, 2014, we connected by Facetime with EOS, an Alaskan FLL team we met last year, to share innovative solutions and demonstrate robots.




Sharing our Robot and Research Solution with Teams Blackout and Category 10 - March 23, 2014

On March 23, 2014, Ctrl-Z hosted FLL teams Blackout and Category 10 to discuss the robot design and the research project. Each of us took turns presenting our research and sharing our robot designs as well as attachments. We wish teams Blackout and Category 10 good luck at the upcoming FIRST LEGO League International Open in June.

Ctrl-Z Awarded a Plaque by the Office of the Fire Marshal and Emergency Management (OFMEM) - May 8, 2014

On May 8, 2014, Jude Kelly and Kalpana Rajgopalan from OFMEM awarded Ctrl-Z with a plaque to recognize our achievements. See the full story in the North York Mirror.

Sharing our Robot and Upright Light with the Chief of Toronto Fire Services, 3 Deputy Chiefs and the District Chief - May 22, 2014

On May 22, 2014, Ctrl-Z visited the Toronto Fire Services Headquarters in North York to present the research project, Upright Light, and robot to the Chief of the Toronto Fire Services, Jim Sales, and members of his senior administration team, Deputy Chief Darrell Reid, Deputy Chief Ron Jenkins, Deputy Chief Matt Pegg and District Chief Peter Derrington. At the conclusion of the meeting, Chief Sales presented Ctrl-Z with a certificate recognizing Ctrl-Z's achievement in the advancement of fire safety.
