Our Experts

Anastasios Venetikidis, University of Toronto

Anastasios has a Bachelor of Sciences degree in geology and a Master of Science in petrology and geochemistry at the University of Thessaloniki, Greece. He is currently a PhD candidate at the department of Earth Sciences at the University of Toronto. Anastasios also has an interest in studying the sedimentary record of ancient river systems with an emphasis on dryland rivers.











Patricia Martel, Natural Hazards and Risk Specialist

Patricia Martel is an experienced natural hazards expert working in the field of emergency management and currently working towards a PhD with a focus of emergency management at the Wilfred Laurier University. An ongoing fascination with hazards has led her to research about their causes, forecasts, warning communication, and the protective action needed to save lives. She is a trained SKYWARN and CANWARN storm spotter and a storm chaser with over 13 years experience. Patricia is interested in finding ways to merge emergency management with the natural social sciences to improve hazard education, warning systems and emergency management programs.








Jude Kelly, Communications and Public Education Officer

Jude is a Communications/Public Education Officer with the Office of the Fire Marshal and Emergency Management (OFMEM). He brings more than 20 years communications and public relations experience to the job. He's worked as a journalist and media officer for a number of Ontario ministries and has won awards from the Saskatchewan Reporters' Association and the Ontario government for outstanding work in journalism and public education. At OFMEM, Jude has performed various duties at the Provincial Emergency Operations Centre, helping coordinate emergency information, social media and key messaging during declared emergencies. Outside of OFMEM, Mr. Kelly is a popular DJ and the music editor of Chill magazine. He is the former chair of the Juno Awards R&B/Soul Committee, and has written or produced for CBC Radio, the National Post, Toronto Star and Word magazine.
